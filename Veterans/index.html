MILITARY LAW STUDENTS

The Ohio State University Moritz College of Law is home to many veteran and active duty service members looking to pursue their JD as another means of serving their nation, state, and local communities. At Ohio State, you will join a community of more than 1,800 current veterans, dependents, active duty, National Guard, and reserve members attending as undergraduate and graduate students. Over 1,400 current faculty and staff veterans; and more than 400 tri-service Air Force, Army, and Navy ROTC are program participants. Both the university and the college have a number of programs in place that are designed to aid current and veteran members of the military as they transition to life as a student and beyond.

The Captain Jonathan D. Grassbaugh Veterans Project

The Captain Jonathan D. Grassbaugh Veterans Project  helps veterans who find themselves in need of legal assistance in the areas of housing and consumer issues.

Ohio GI Promise

The Ohio GI Promise seeks to make Ohio the most veteran-friendly state in the country for higher education.


Vets4Vets

The Vets 4 Vets mission is to support and assist veterans, service members, and their families in their transition upon completion of service.


Veteran Housing

The Veterans House is a homelike housing option for students who are veterans, active duty, reserves, and National Guard.


Yellow Ribbon Program

The Yellow Ribbon Program allows institutions of higher learning in the U.S. to voluntarily enter into an agreement with the VA to fund tuition and fee expenses that exceed the highest public in-state undergraduate tuition and fee rate in their state.
