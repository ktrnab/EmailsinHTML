The Ohio State University Moritz College of Law Mayhew-Hite Report


An occasional newsletter highlighting recent and upcoming activities and achievements of the Moritz faculty

ACCOLADES
 

Doug Berman is the 2018 Recipient of the Richard P. Kern Memorial Award, awarded by the National Association of Sentencing Commissions for his contributions to the development of sentencing policy and research. Congratulations, Doug!

Ellen Deason was awarded the AALS Section of Dispute Resolution Best ADR Article of 2017, presented at AALS ADR Section Works-in-Progress conference this past October. Congratulations, Ellen!

Nancy Rogers was awarded a Lifetime Achievement Award by the International Academy of Mediators in October for her work and impact in the field of dispute resolution. Congratulations, Nancy!

PRESENTATIONS
 

Akbar at Princeton. Amna Akbar is currently a LAPA fellow at Princeton where she recently presented her paper “(Arguments for) Police Abolition.” She also presented on the topic of political revolution as part of the Praxis 13/13 series facilitated by Columbia Law School's Bernard Harcourt and his Center for Contemporary Critical Thought.

Quigley in Singapore. John Quigley was in Singapore in December for The Vietnam and Arab-Israeli Conflicts: International Legal Migrations, Comparisons, and Connections; he presented “Legality of Military Action by Egypt and Syria in October 1973” for the panel on the Arab-Israeli Conflict.

Rudesill in D.C. Dakota Rudesill was in Washington, D.C. for two events in October. He was a participant in the Roundtable on Presidential Nuclear Launch Authority sponsored by the Nuclear Threat Initiative, and later presented “Learning-by-Doing: Training the Next Generation of Professionals” at the U.S. Air Force Association’ 7th Annual Triad Conference.

Shane Testifies Before Congress. Peter Shane testified before the Senate Judiciary Committee in September on the nomination of Brett Kavanaugh to the U.S. Supreme Court, discussing Kavanaugh’s approach to constitutional questions of executive power and presidential accountability.

Walker in Washington. Chris Walker presented research at the Federalist Society’s 2018 National Lawyers Convention, serving on the panel Rulemaking by Adjudication: Who Am I to Judge?



PUBLICATIONS & MEDIA
 

Doug Berman on Drug Policy. Doug Berman was a guest on WOSU’s All Sides with Ann Fisher, where he discussed the First Step Act, a federal criminal justice reform bill that would alter mandatory minimums for non-violent drug offenses, and the possibility of a sentencing reform in Ohio. He also recorded Episode 149 of the Scholars Strategy Network podcast, “Beyond Legal Marijuana.”

Colker on Transgender Rights. Ruth Colker appeared on WOSU’s All Sides with Ann Fisher and wrote a Jurist comment on the Trump Administration’s actions to roll back the rights of transgender people.

Rogers’ New Edition. The second edition of Nancy Rogers’ textbook Designing Systems and Processes for Managing Disputes has just been published by Wolters Kluwer; the new edition has examples from community division and even the story of a Brazilian federal judge who used the first edition of the textbook to resolve some consolidated complex litigation stemming from the creation of a new national park.

Rudesill on Nuclear Policy. Dakota Rudesill was interviewed in June on “Nuclear Tensions, Nuclear Weapons, and a Long History of Nuclear War” for the NPR podcast Origins: Current Events in Historical Perspective. He also has recently published two articles: “Trump’s Secret Order on Pulling the Cyber Trigger,” and “Nuclear Arms Control After Helsinki: If Trump and Putin Want a Deal on Arms Control, Here’s Where to Start.”
